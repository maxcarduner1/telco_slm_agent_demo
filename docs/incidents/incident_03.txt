EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

On April 15, 2026, the Mountain West region experienced a critical network incident characterized by excessive ping-pong handovers between overlapping 5G New Radio (NR) and Long Term Evolution (LTE) cells. The incident resulted in widespread Voice over LTE (VoLTE) call drops and degraded user experience across multiple cell sites in the Denver, Salt Lake City, and Phoenix metropolitan areas.

The root cause was identified as overly aggressive A3 offset parameters in the handover algorithm, combined with insufficient hysteresis settings and inadequate Time-to-Trigger (TTT) values. These configuration parameters caused User Equipment (UE) to rapidly oscillate between serving cells, creating a handover storm that overwhelmed network resources and disrupted voice services.

The incident was resolved through emergency parameter adjustments including hysteresis increase from 1dB to 3dB and TTT extension from 160ms to 320ms. Total customer impact affected approximately 2.3 million subscribers with an estimated revenue impact of $1.2 million.

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS

Primary Analysis Method: 5-Whys Methodology

Why 1: Why did customers experience dropped VoLTE calls?
Answer: Excessive ping-pong handovers between NR and LTE cells caused connection instability and call termination.

Why 2: Why were there excessive ping-pong handovers?
Answer: User Equipment rapidly switched between serving cells due to marginal signal conditions and aggressive handover triggering.

Why 3: Why did UE rapidly switch between cells?
Answer: A3 offset parameters were configured too aggressively, causing premature handover decisions with minimal signal strength differences.

Why 4: Why were A3 offset parameters configured aggressively?
Answer: Recent network optimization focused on maximizing throughput led to reduced handover thresholds without adequate consideration of stability margins.

Why 5: Why was stability not adequately considered during optimization?
Answer: The parameter change process lacked comprehensive impact assessment for dual connectivity scenarios and insufficient hysteresis/TTT safeguards were implemented.

Contributing Factors Analysis:

Technical Factors:
- Hysteresis setting of 1dB provided insufficient margin for signal fluctuations
- TTT value of 160ms was too short for stable handover decisions
- Overlapping cell coverage areas created ambiguous serving cell selection
- RF propagation variations in mountainous terrain exacerbated signal instability

Process Factors:
- Parameter optimization process focused primarily on throughput metrics
- Limited testing of dual connectivity handover scenarios
- Insufficient validation of parameter interactions between NR and LTE layers
- Inadequate monitoring of handover stability metrics during optimization

Environmental Factors:
- Mountainous terrain created challenging RF propagation conditions
- Weather conditions on incident day included atmospheric ducting effects
- High subscriber density during morning peak hours amplified impact

LESSONS LEARNED

Technical Lessons:

1. Dual connectivity handover optimization requires careful balance between performance and stability, with stability taking precedence in challenging RF environments.

2. Hysteresis and TTT parameters serve as critical stability safeguards and should not be minimized without thorough impact analysis.

3. Mountainous terrain creates unique propagation challenges that require specialized parameter tuning approaches different from urban environments.

4. Real-time monitoring of handover stability metrics is essential for early detection of optimization-induced issues.

Process Lessons:

1. Parameter optimization activities must include comprehensive stability impact assessment beyond throughput-focused metrics.

2. Change management processes should mandate testing in representative network conditions before production deployment.

3. Cross-functional collaboration between optimization, operations, and customer experience teams is critical for balanced network performance.

4. Incident response procedures should include rapid parameter rollback capabilities for configuration-related issues.

Organizational Lessons:

1. Network optimization objectives should balance multiple performance dimensions including stability, quality, and customer experience.

2. Investment in predictive analytics and automated monitoring capabilities provides significant value for preventing similar incidents.

3. Regular review and validation of parameter optimization strategies helps identify potential risks before they impact customers.

4. Customer communication during network incidents should be proactive and transparent to maintain trust and satisfaction.
